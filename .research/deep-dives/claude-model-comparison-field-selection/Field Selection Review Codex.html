Architecture Overview

The heart of the decision is simple: field selection is a presentation concern. The winning branch is the one that keeps it closest to presentation.

agent-904b6be1

Caller Requests a smaller response
FastMCP Tool Accepts fields and null controls
OperatorService Performs projection inside the service and returns dict-shaped items
Result Works, but schema quality is lower because item shape becomes generic

agent-1edcddf2

Caller Requests specific fields
FastMCP Tool Owns the final response contract

agent-878a38d0

FastMCP Tool Mostly unchanged outwardly

Why This Branch Is The Best

The final recommendation is not subtle. The winning branch is the only one that stays clean at every level that matters.

It respects the architecture

Projection belongs at the presentation boundary. agent-1edcddf2 is the only branch that follows that principle consistently.

It respects the contract

The service continues to return full Task and Project models. Internal callers do not have to guess whether the result shape changed.

It respects MCP clients

The output schema is relaxed instead of abandoned. Clients still see what fields exist, and projected responses remain structurally valid.

Why the runner-up loses

agent-904b6be1 is direct and disciplined, but it pays for that simplicity by making the response schema less informative than it needs to be.

Why third place stays third

agent-878a38d0 passes targeted tests, but the service signature stops being fully honest once projected dict-like items are returned behind model-like types.

The shortest possible conclusion

agent-1edcddf2 is the only implementation that solves the feature and the contract implications of the feature.

Targeted source-pinned test runs were used during the review with each worktree importing its own src/ tree. The choice therefore comes down to architecture quality and contract integrity, not to one branch simply failing outright.
